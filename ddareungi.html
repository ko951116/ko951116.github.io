Ddareungi App Redesign
Improving the UX/UI of Ddareungi app.

Project Overview

Ddareungi is a shared bicycle service operated by the Seoul Metropolitan Government, which has been widely used since its launch in 2015. It is so famous and familiar service that any Seoul citizen has heard of it at least once.

However, people's assessment of this application is low. As a user of Ddareungi, I also sometimes felt uncomfortable with the application. Therefore, I worked on redesign to improve the UX and UI of Ddareungi application.

Duration

Apr 2020 (2-3weeks)

Service Analysis

The number of users of Ddareungi continues to increase, but the application rating continues to drop. Thus, even though it has been about five years since the launch of the app, users continue to feel uncomfortable using the app. So I decided to redesign this app to simplify it.

Key Problems: What makes using the app difficult?

1Infection Risk

Incorrect disposal harmful to animals and humans as the virus can survive on a plastic surgical mask for about seven days. There is the fear that PP exposed to COVID-19 could infect the front-line waste management workers who may not be properly outfitted or take the necessary precautions to handle potentially hazardous waste.

2Materials

The most commonly used material for a disposable mask is polypropylene(PP), a polymer derived from petroleum oil. In addition to PP non-woven fabric, the mask includes different materials, so it can be properly regenerated only when these are removed.

3Lack of Awareness

For recycling, used masks should be dumped separately and disposed not in general waste. However, most people throw their masks into a general trash can or even throw them on the ground at random.

2. Ideation

Who is the main target?

Based on the user survey, I was able to group the main targets into three groups. There is an arbitrary group of users who take bicycles for any reason, such as an emergency (Group 01: Random Users). A group of ordinary users is a group that regularly uses bicycles to commute to and from work and school(Group 02: Regular Users). A sports user group is a group that uses bicycles for sports or sports purposes(Group 03: Sports Users).

UX Workflow & Pain points

To visualize the current workflow of the app and point the pain points, I draw a flow model regarding each target group. After that, I put the possible solutions based on the key problems for each user.

Solution Ideas

Solution 01: Automatic Login & Password

1Automatic Login

Regular users and sports users are people who use bicycles regularly. Therefore, logging in every time can be very cumbersome. Automatic login function is required.

2Password

For random users, it is burdensome to enter a password when they first sign up. In a situation where a bicycle is urgently needed, there may be a lack of time to set a password carefully, and it may be embarrassing to forget the password when the bike needs to be used again later. Therefore, every time a bicycle is rented, it is changed to a temporary password of four digits.






Solution 02: Directions

Random users who urgently need bicycles to their destinations and sports users who want to exercise their bikes on the course they want have to use different directions applications. It is not only inconvenient to check back and forth between two applications, but also dangerous while riding a bicycle, as the Ddareungi app needs to be constantly checked to check the remaining time. The Ddareungi app needs a directions function to the destination.






Solution 03: Exercise Information

Sports users want to check their records of exercising while riding the Ddareungi. Currently, there is no such function, so users use another health record app, but it is difficult to see exactly how much they exercised with just a bicycle because the Ddareungi is not exclusive. Therefore, it is necessary to display the driving time, distance, and calories consumed in real time, and to collect the data and check it on a monthly or daily basis.






Solution 04: Alarm & Extending the remaining time

1Alarm

Currently, when using the Ddareungi, the alarm is indirectly notified through Kakao Talk 10 minutes before the remaining time. The problem is, some people do not use Kakao Talk, and people do not easily recognize that it is an alarm when riding because it is not an alarm that comes from the app itself. Therefore, the Ddareungi app gives an alarm five minutes before returning it with a loud vibration or sound on its own so that users can easily recognize the remaining time while even riding.

2Extend the rental time

When the alarm goes off as shown above, users can extend it on the app if they want to. However, it is only possible to extend it once for the smooth sharing of bicycles among citizens.

3. Design Prototype

Information Architecture

The information architecture of the service is consists of a login page, a map, an alarm and buying a ticket. Once the user logs in, the user can see a map to check his or her location and the location of the bicycle rental station. The map allows the user to perform a variety of functions, of which clicking Buy Ticket will switch to the screen for the purchase of the ticket. When the rental time approaches, it automatically switches to the alarm screen.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

Mid-Fidelity Prototype

I improved design ideas and details that need to be addressed. Then I built mid-fidelity prototypes to visualize key functionalities of the app.

Reflections

Collaboration with people of various backgrounds.
It was not only about learning a holistic design process but an opportunity to work together with various people from different background. We all have a different background such as engineering, industrial design and UX design and even our nationalities were different like South Korea, Hong Kong, Indonesia and Australia. It was such a wonderful experiences to work with different people. It was my first time collaborating with an industrial design major, and for me, who liked design and learning, the process of collaborating with industrial design team members was enjoyable and I could learn many new things.

Make the world a better place.
The greatest part of this project was that we enjoyed the project itself. In this pandemic, we were happy that we tried to create something useful for human kind. Although we know that our project may be difficult to realize in reality due to various constraints, it was a project that started with the hope that our project would actually be realized in the near future and solve the problem of disposable masks around the world. This project made me more interested in understanding and solving problems in the world.

Cooperation in the COVID-19 pandemic.
The camp, which was held during the Pandemics season due to COVID-19, was held online. All meetings, except field trips and 3D prototyping, were conducted online (mostly through Zoom and MIRO), and so sometimes there were misunderstandings between each other. But for about two weeks, we gradually learned how to collaborate and work with colleagues in this pandemic era. I think it was a good opportunity to learn directly how to collaborate with colleagues even through online.
