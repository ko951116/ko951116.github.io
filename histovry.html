Histovry
Gyeongbokgung Palace VR tour application.

Project Overview

With the rise of VR technology, more realistic experiences are possible, and tourism using these virtual reality contents is having a positive impact on the revitalization of the local tourism industry.

So we started to develop a program to explore historical sites through VR, which allows us to tour 'Gyeongbokgung Palace' in Seoul by incorporating VR technology into tourism.

The Process

1. Research

Inspiration

As K-pop becomes popular, foreigners' interest in Korean culture is increasing, and therefore the number of travelers is increasing. However, depending on the circumstances, they may not be able to come to Korea directly, so we started the project to solve this problem.

Problems: What makes traveling to Seoul difficult?

1Place Constraint

Thanks to the popularity of K-pop, more and more people want to travel to Korea, but it is not easy to come to Korea from a country far from Asia. Of course, it is also not easy to travel from provinces to Seoul in Korea depending on the situation.

2Time

It is not easy to travel for a long time because most people work or go to school. In particular, students and office workers do not have much time to travel.

3Money

Flight tickets, hotel reservations, etc. are essential to travel, but it can be burdensome to pay for everything. In particular, students are likely to have limited money to spend.

Development Environment

The overall project development environment is Unity, and the development language is C#. To implement the login system, a script was written in C# language inside Unity and it is connected to a PHP file, and then the contents of the Web DB were imported. As an application development tool, we used Android Studio 3.0 version SDK and Java JDK1.8.0_172. Target devices are Android operating system devices.

Sign Up & Login System

We needed a server to manage the user's information, so we bought a 64bit JSP light-hosting from Cafe 24 (www.cafe24.com). Sign up and login pages are implemented using Unity's UI. We used C# scripts and php files to access the server's db, enabling login and sign up.

Scene Transition

C# scripts are inserted so that users can gaze at where they want to tour for more than 2 seconds and move on to the desired scene.

VR Tour Scene

The login screen is implemented as 2D for user convenience, and the Cardboard is added after selecting None in the Virtual Reality SDKs component in order to implement it as a VR mode from the next screen. VRNoThanks script was added to the login scene so that it could be seen on a regular screen, while VRYesPlease script was added to the main menu scene, the second scene, to make all subsequent scenes work in VR mode.




4. Final design

Solution: We have a solution!

We have seen our errors in the past with the use of single use plastics and not considering end of life of products. Designing a solution that remediates the forthcoming issue ever-growing volume of used face mask is something that needs to be addressed immediately before it becomes insurmountable.

What are we offering?

1Reverse Vending Machine

Innovative RVM turns contaminated masks into 100% sterilized material. This material can be used by the public or in industrial application.

2App

App to connect users to machines, incentives and rewards. Capture a QR Code to Return and Save Masks Easily. Easy to use the interface and connect to the vending machine.

3DIY Kits

DIY experience for the public to creatively repurpose the material as fun, novel and useful products.




Final Design:

Contactless way to safely dispose of used masks in a way that rewards and protects wearers while also giving the masks a purposeful second life. Fun and approachable in a time of pessimism and uncertainty.




Final Design:

Fun, optimistic and engaging DIY projects that reinforce sustainable values and a unique approach to prevent an improper disposal of used masks.

Reflections

The camp, which was held during the Pandemics season due to COVID-19, was held online. All meetings, except field trips and prototyping, were conducted online, and so sometimes there were misunderstandings between each other. But for about two weeks, we gradually learned how to collaborate and work with colleagues in this Corona era. Personally, I think it was a good opportunity to learn directly how to collaborate with colleagues in these situations.

It was not only about learning a design process but an opportunity to work together with various people from different background. We all have a different background such as engineering, industrial design and UX design and even our nationalities were different like South Korea, Hong Kong, Indonesia and Australia. It was such a wonderful experiences to work with different people. It was my first time collaborating with an industrial design major, and for me, who liked design and learning, the process of collaborating with industrial design team members was enjoyable and I could learn many new things.

The great part of this project was that we enjoyed the working process. In this pandemic, we were happy that we tried to create something useful for human kind. Although we know that our project may be difficult to realize in reality due to various constraints, it was a project that started with the hope that our project would actually be realized in the near future and solve the problem of disposable masks around the world.

I was the team leader of Team 8 from the start. Obviously, it was never easy to pull people from different backgrounds, including nationality and major, into one team. However, all the team members faithfully performed their duties in the fields they could do, making it relatively easy to lead the team in a good direction. I think this good teamwork, along with good project results, contributed to winning the Best Project Award at the end of the camp. Once again, thank the team members from all over the world for working together on the project.
