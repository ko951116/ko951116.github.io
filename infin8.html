A holistic integrated system to solve the wastage problem caused by the disposal of face masks.

Project Overview

In 2020, the coronavirus pandemic occured in the world. As the situation became more serious, many people wore face masks to stop the spread of the coronavirus. Face masks are part of our daily lives during this pandemic. Many of them are designed to be used just once, which means thousands of tonnes of waste.

Every month some 129 billion disposable masks are now being consumed in the world due to the spread of the coronavirus pandemic. They have worsened the amount of plastic polluting the world’s landfill and oceans. Disposable masks contain plastics which pollute water and can harm wildlife who eat them or become tangled in them.

As a solution for these issues, we designed a creative and holistic integrated system to solve the wastage problem caused by the improper disposal of single use face masks.

2Desktop Research Study

As you can see from the picture below, the masks we use consist of layers made of different materials. Among them, Polypropylene(PP) is one of the most commonly used types of plastics, which can actually be recycled. The problem is that in the case of masks, layers of various materials overlap, so it is difficult to extract only recyclable PP materials and recycle them properly. Therefore, experts believe that while recycling of masks may be technically possible, it is not economically feasible to recycle them as high value-added products.

2. Ideation

Key Criteria

With these considerations for all the previous research steps, we were able to narrow down the key criteria for our holistic system for the waste of the disposable face masks.

Exploring Design Ideas

Step1: How to collect the masks?

To collect and recycle masks, you first have to decide how to collect them. We decided to design a device to collect used masks, and explored it in various directions.

Considerations

  • Sterilizing bin to collect and treat used masks
  • Could be small product be retrofit to home bins?
  • Separated components can be safely treated at industrial scale and repurposed
  • Could be made of this repurposed mask material
  • Designated option for disposal of masks (addresses pain point)
  • Sterilizing (UV, Steam or heat)
  • Could be positioned at areas where people regularly attend (Grocery store, Outside train station)

Initial Design Ideas




Vending Machine Design Ideas

Combining various early sketches and considerations, there seemed to be a problem that letting people sterilize or separate masks on their own at home would not only reduce participation rates but also complicate the distribution process. Furthermore, since the device is only one step in our project and is not the final goal, considering the steps after collecting the mask, the device used at home seemed to be constrained. Thus we decided on a device in the form of a large vending machine installed for anyone to use outdoors. After selecting the machine form, we started a research on vending machines and began brainstorming design ideas.

And we decided not only to collect the mask, but also to design a system that will allow it to be returned to the person who put it after a complete sterilization and cutting process for recycling products.

Journey Map

Design Objectives

1Device: for collecting & returning the masks

Make a device that keeps people from throwing away masks anywhere. Suppose it has a complete sterilization system inside the device. To reduce people's concerns about infections, make it a system that does not recycle masks worn by others, but only those used by themselves.

2Product: resultant product

Give DIY kits to people to make a small product who spend most of their time at home because of the COVID-19. It could be educational and fun.

3App: connecting people with the device and the product

Give people incentives for recycling the masks through the app. Connect users to the machine and the product with easy UX/UI.

Final Concept

Educate the public:

1. Safely dispose used masks through a specially designed vending machine

2. Turn used mask materials into a functional products via various DIY Kits

3. Design Prototype

Since our solution is a holistic system that includes three parts of the vending machine, DIY Kits, and an application, we conducted the prototyping by the three steps below.





STEP 01: Reverse Vending Machine Prototyping

The reverse vending machine will be located on the street. It has a QR code on the front for the scan with the app. The vending machine should have the functions of sterilization and shredding. The user can get the sterilized, chopped mask sheets from the vending machine and can use them to make a small DIY product.




STEP 02: DIY Kits prototyping

The reason for making this prototype was to experience the process of making the useful items using the masks which is the overall purpose of the project. It was also because we had to know what the results of our project would look like and what people will experience in the process of making it.

Rose Mold

We made a rose shaped DIY mockup with resin and mask pieces, may work for our DIY idea. We could control the resin setting time by the proportion of the fixative.

Plant Pot Mockup

We made a plant pot mockup with masks and whiteglue. It was for trial and record in our workshop process. Also, it was a great hands-on experience for us.

STEP 03: Application

The user can use the app to get the points by scanning the QR code on the reverse vending machine after putting the used masks. The user can get a discount with the points when buying a DIY kit on the DIY shop on the app. It has many other features to connect the user and the reverse vending machine.

4. Final Design

Solution

Innovative RVM turns used masks into 100% sterilized material. This material can be used by the public or in industrial application.

2App

An app to connect users to machines, incentives and rewards. Scan the QR code with the app to return and save masks Easily. Easy to use the interface and connect to the vending machine.

I think this good teamwork, along with good project results, contributed to displaying the project at the DK festival. Once again, thank the team members for working together on the project.
