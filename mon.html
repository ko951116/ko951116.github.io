Project Overview

In 2020, the coronavirus pandemic occured in the world. As the situation became more serious, many people wore face masks to stop the spread of the coronavirus. Face masks are part of our daily lives during this pandemic. Many of them are designed to be used just once, which means thousands of tonnes of waste.

Every month some 129 billion disposable masks are now being consumed in the world due to the spread of the coronavirus pandemic. They have worsened the amount of plastic polluting the world’s landfill and oceans. Disposable masks contain plastics which pollute water and can harm wildlife who eat them or become tangled in them.

STEP 01

Situation: Find the shortest path to exit the maze

Let's say there are no clues whether 1 is Jiyoon's house or 2. Anyway, it is the most closest one from the point where MON starts. How might MON find the destination?

BFS Algorithm

View Code

1Apply all possible operators (sucessor function) to the start node

2Apply all possible operators to all the direct successors of the start node

3Apply all possible operators to their successors till goal node found

Result

Found Jiyoon's house within 18steps!

STEP 02

Situation:

Step1: How to collect the masks?

To collect and recycle masks, you first have to decide how to collect them. We decided to design a device to collect used masks, and explored it in various directions.

Considerations

  • Sterilizing bin to collect and treat used masks
  • Could be small product be retrofit to home bins?
  • Separated components can be safely treated at industrial scale and repurposed
  • Could be made of this repurposed mask material
  • Designated option for disposal of masks (addresses pain point)
  • Sterilizing (UV, Steam or heat)
  • Could be positioned at areas where people regularly attend (Grocery store, Outside train station)

Initial Design Ideas




Vending Machine Design Ideas

Combining various early sketches and considerations, there seemed to be a problem that letting people sterilize or separate masks on their own at home would not only reduce participation rates but also complicate the distribution process. Furthermore, since the device is only one step in our project and is not the final goal, considering the steps after collecting the mask, the device used at home seemed to be constrained. Thus we decided on a device in the form of a large vending machine installed for anyone to use outdoors. After selecting the machine form, we started a research on vending machines and began brainstorming design ideas.

And we decided not only to collect the mask, but also to design a system that will allow it to be returned to the person who put it after a complete sterilization and cutting process for recycling products.

Journey Map

Design Objectives

1Device: for collecting & returning the masks

Make a device that keeps people from throwing away masks anywhere. Suppose it has a complete sterilization system inside the device. To reduce people's concerns about infections, make it a system that does not recycle masks worn by others, but only those used by themselves.

2Product: resultant product

Give DIY kits to people to make a small product who spend most of their time at home because of the COVID-19. It could be educational and fun.

3App: connecting people with the device and the product

Give people incentives for recycling the masks through the app. Connect users to the machine and the product with easy UX/UI.

Final Concept

Educate the public:

1. Safely dispose used masks through a specially designed vending machine

2. Turn used mask materials into a functional products via various DIY Kits

Step 04

Solution

What are we offering?

1Reverse Vending Machine

Innovative RVM turns contaminated masks into 100% sterilized material. This material can be used by the public or in industrial application.

2App

App to connect users to machines, incentives and rewards. Scan the QR code with the app to return and save masks Easily. Easy to use the interface and connect to the vending machine.

3DIY Kits

DIY experience for the public to creatively repurpose the material as fun, novel and useful products.




UX Workflow

We added an industrial functional process at the end, taking into account people who do not want to recycle masks themselves.







Final Design: Reverse Vending Machine

Contactless way to safely dispose of used masks in a way that rewards and protects wearers while also giving the masks a purposeful second life. Fun and approachable in a time of pessimism and uncertainty.

Reflections

Collaboration with people of various backgrounds.
It was not only about learning a holistic design process but an opportunity to work together with various people from different background. We all have a different background such as engineering, industrial design and UX design and even our nationalities were different like South Korea, Hong Kong, Indonesia and Australia. It was such a wonderful experiences to work with different people. It was my first time collaborating with an industrial design major, and for me, who liked design and learning, the process of collaborating with industrial design team members was enjoyable and I could learn many new things.

Make the world a better place.
The greatest part of this project was that we enjoyed the project itself. In this pandemic, we were happy that we tried to create something useful for human kind. Although we know that our project may be difficult to realize in reality due to various constraints, it was a project that started with the hope that our project would actually be realized in the near future and solve the problem of disposable masks around the world. This project made me more interested in understanding and solving problems in the world.

Cooperation in the COVID-19 pandemic.
The camp, which was held during the Pandemics season due to COVID-19, was held online. All meetings, except field trips and 3D prototyping, were conducted online (mostly through Zoom and MIRO), and so sometimes there were misunderstandings between each other. But for about two weeks, we gradually learned how to collaborate and work with colleagues in this pandemic era. I think it was a good opportunity to learn directly how to collaborate with colleagues even through online.

As a team leader.
I was the team leader of Team 8 from the start. Obviously, it was never easy to pull people from different backgrounds, including nationality and major, into one team. However, all the team members faithfully performed their duties in the fields they could do, making it relatively easy to lead the team in a good direction. I think this good teamwork, along with good project results, contributed to displaying the project at the DK festival. Once again, thank the team members for working together on the project.
